Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 17, 2026

1. Who we are

Barth Ad Launch (“Barth”) is an advertising management tool that uses the Meta Marketing API to create and manage ad campaigns on behalf of authorized business accounts. The operator of this application is the data controller for the data described below.

2. What data we collect and why

We collect and use only the data necessary to provide the service:

  • Account and API credentials: Meta ad account IDs and access tokens, stored in the business operator’s own systems, to connect to the Meta Marketing API.
  • Campaign data: Ad campaign names, budgets, targeting settings, and creative content (e.g. video and image files) that you provide when creating or managing ads. This data is sent to Meta via the Marketing API to run your campaigns.
  • Optional brief text: Any campaign or creative brief you enter in the app, used only to generate ad copy and manage campaigns.

We do not collect personal data from people who see your ads on Meta platforms (e.g. Facebook, Instagram). Meta’s own policies apply to data collected on their platforms.

3. How we use your data

Your data is used solely to:

  • Connect to Meta’s Marketing API and manage your ad accounts.
  • Create, edit, and run ad campaigns, ad sets, and creatives as you instruct.
  • Generate ad copy (e.g. captions) when you use that feature.

We do not sell your data. We do not use it for advertising or profiling beyond what is required to operate the service.

4. Data sharing and third parties

We share data only as needed to provide the service:

  • Meta (Facebook): Campaign data, creatives, and credentials are sent to Meta via the Marketing API in accordance with Meta’s Data Policy and our use of their API.
  • AI provider (Anthropic): When you use caption generation, your brief and context may be sent to Anthropic’s Claude service to generate ad copy. See Anthropic’s privacy policy.

We do not share your data with other third parties for their marketing purposes.

5. Data retention and storage

Configuration and credentials are stored in the business operator’s environment for as long as the service is used. We do not retain a separate copy of your Meta account data beyond what is needed to fulfill API requests. Logs may be kept for a limited time for security and troubleshooting.

6. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, or delete your data, or to object to or restrict certain processing. To exercise these rights or ask questions about your data, contact us using the details in Section 9.

7. Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your data (e.g. secure storage of credentials and use of HTTPS). No system is completely secure; you provide credentials and data at your own risk.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will be revised when we do. Continued use of the service after changes means you accept the updated policy.

9. Contact

For privacy-related questions or requests, contact the business that operates this app at the email address listed in your Meta app settings, or via the contact method provided when you signed up for the service.
